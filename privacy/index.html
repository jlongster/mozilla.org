Privacy Principles

There are some basic principles regarding user privacy that Mozilla believes should guide our decision making where user information may be collected. These are:

  • Transparency & No surprises. Only use and share collected information for user benefit and as disclosed
  • Give the user actionable and informed choices by informing & educating at the point of collection/engagement and providing a choice to opt-out
  • Sensible default settings that balance safety and user experience as appropriate for the context of the transaction
  • Collect and retain the least amount of information necessary for the feature or task
  • Do not disclose personal user information without consent
  • Only when it benefits the web, users, or developers, share anonymous aggregate data
  • Make privacy a factor when selecting and interacting with 3rd parties

Below is more information on how we carry out these principles.

Product Features

Want to know about the privacy and security features of Firefox? Check out the Firefox Features page for more info.

If you're thinking of using Sync so you can access your history, passwords, bookmarks and open tabs across all your devices, you may be wondering if your information will remain private. The Sync privacy policy explains what information is collected or received by Mozilla when you use Sync, and how that information is handled.

We built our Test Pilot add-on, which allows Firefox users to participate in studies that help us improve Firefox, with privacy in mind. Find out more in the Test Pilot FAQs.

Privacy in Action

During the summer of 2010, Mozilla brought in outside consultants to conduct a review of our privacy practices. This was a fantastically useful project that left us with concrete actions to take to improve our practices. We have completed some of those actions and are still working on others.

  • Summary of our requirements for the protection of user data that we send to our potential vendors.
  • Privacy checklist we recently began using when starting a project. It's a work in progress.
  • Our IT security team conducts a security review of all vendors we are considering working with who would have access to any personal information of our users.
