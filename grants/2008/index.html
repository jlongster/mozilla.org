In 2008, our grants program focused heavily on accessibility initiatives.

Summary of 2008 Grants

  • Accessibility: $317,355
  • Community: $187,625
  • Education: $0
  • Open Source: $25,000
  • Total: $514,995

Accessibility

  • $70,000 to the Adaptive Technology Resource Centre at the University of Toronto to promote ARIA adoption and DHTML accessibility
  • $25,000 to Benetech to develop an open source, browser-based screen reader
  • $25,000 to Vquence to produce a study and recommendations on the current state of video accessibility technology
  • $24,000 to The Paciello Group for Phase II of the Firebug Accessibility Project
  • $20,000 to Eitan Isaacson to develop non-interactive inspection of the accessibility API provided by a web browser for ARIA examples
  • $20,000 to The Paciello Group to develop a Firefox/Mozilla accessibility reporting panel and ARIA QA extension
  • $20,000 to Scott Haeger and $25,000 to Eitan Isaacson to enhance the Orca open source screen reader
  • $15,000 to Thomas Logan to document differences in ARIA implementations
  • $15,000 to Project:Possibility
  • $10,000 to Alexander Surkov to develop automated tests and regression fixes in Mozilla accessibility module
  • $15,000 to Alexander Surkov to implement the IAccessible2 accessibility API for Windows
  • $14,000 to Steve Lee for the Jambu project
  • $8,855 to Ben Millard to study how authors use HTML in order to help HTML5 be more accessible
  • $5,000 to Codethink Ltd to evaluate the feasibility of porting the GNOME AT-SPI accessibility interface from ORBIT to D-Bus
  • $3,500 to Knowbility to sponsor accessibility training at the 2008 LEAD conference
  • $1,000 to CIFAL Atlanta to support publication and distribution of the G3ict book "The Accessibility Imperative"
  • $1,000 to Web4All to sponsor the W4 2009 conference on web accessibility

Community Grants

  • $100,000 to Creative Commons to support general activities of Creative Commons
  • $30,000 to the Mozdev Community Organization
  • $10,000 to CompuMentor to sponsor the 2008 NetSquared conference
  • $10,000 to the Software Freedom Law Center to support Software Freedom Conservancy activities
  • $5,000 to Annodex Association to sponsor the Foundations of Open Media Software Workshop
  • $5,000 to the Berkeley Center for Law and Technology to sponsor the Free Culture 2008 Conference
  • $5,000 to TeX Users Group to support development of the Open Font Library
  • $5,000 to Everything Solved, Inc., to conduct a survey of desired Bugzilla features
  • $4,500 to Wellington Fernando de Macedo to add support for HTML5 Server-Sent DOM Events to Mozilla
  • $2,000 to UK Citizens Online Democracy to sponsor the mySociety fifth anniversary celebration
  • $10,000 to the Personal Democracy Forum to sponsor the 2008 PdF conference
  • $1,125 to Peter Jaros to improve scriptability in the Camino browser

Open Source Grants

  • $25,000 to Atlan Laboratories to support FIPS 140-2 validation
