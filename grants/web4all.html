The Web4All Conference is an annual cross-disciplinary gathering focused on Scientific Enquiry, Research, Development, and Engineering. The aim is to focus on accessibility by encouraging participation from many disciplines. Views bridge academia, commerce, and industry and arguments encompassing a range of beliefs across the design-accessibility spectrum are presented.

Grant Summary

  • 2010: $1,000
  • 2009: $1,000
  • 2008: $1,000
  • 2007: $1,000
  • Focus: Open Source
  • Recipient: World Wide Web Consortium
  • Location: United Kingdom

More Information

Mozilla has been a Web4All Conference Sponsor for several years, and has also sponsored several speakers to be able to attend. This gathering is important for its broad mission of accessibility and the way it brings together members of the accessibility community to work together.

Web4All

Web4All is a non-profit based in the United Kingdom dedicated to improving software accessibility. It is governed by individuals from all over the globe, including individuals from commercial software, academia, and public interest organizations.

Please consider making a contribution to the Mozilla Foundation so we can continue to make grants to other worthwhile organizations such as Web4All.
