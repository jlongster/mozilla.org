This grant provided sponsorship for the Free Culture Conference put on by the Berkeley Center for Law and Technology, held October 11 and 12, 2008 in Berkeley, California.

Grant Summary

  • 2008: $5,000
  • Focus: Education
  • Recipient: Berkeley Center for Law and Technology
  • Location: United States

More Information

The Free Culture Conference is a yearly touchstone event for the advancement of free cultures where all members are free to participate without artificial limits on who can participate or in what way. The free culture movement promotes creativity, innovation, communication, free expression, public access to knowledge, and citizens' civil liberties.

The Berkeley Center for Law and Technology

The BLCT was founded in 1995 to foster beneficial and ethical advancement of technology by promoting the understanding and guiding the development of intellectual property and related fields of law and policy as they intersect with business, science and technology.
