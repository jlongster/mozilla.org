This grant provides funding to help the Software Freedom Conservancy serve additional open source projects and work more closely with peer projects.

Grant Summary

  • 2009: $10,000
  • 2008: $10,000
  • Focus: Open Source
  • Recipient: Software Freedom Conservancy
  • Location: United States

More Information

The Mozilla Foundation provided a $10,000 grant to the Software Freedom Conservancy in 2008. During the grant period, Mozilla's funding helped the Conservancy to provide administrative, financial management, coordination and logistical services to twenty FLOSS (Free, Libre, and Open Source Software) projects including Foresight Linux, Sugar Labs, jQuery, Amarok, Darcs, OpenInkpot, and K-3D. The Conservancy began an effort to organize the development of financial management software for use by itself and other nonprofit organizations associated with FLOSS.

Software Freedom Conservancy

The Software Freedom Conservancy is a sister organization to the Software Freedom Law Center. The two organizations share staff and offices but are independent entities. Both SFLC and SFC offer services to all free software and open source projects.

Please consider making a contribution to the Mozilla Foundation so we can continue to make grants to other worthwhile organizations such as the Software Freedom Conservancy.
