Email is a critical tool in the workplace and arguably more so for users who are blind. These users typically have to resort to paying for expensive, proprietary email software. Thunderbird should be considered an exemplar of email accessibility and FLOSS choice for users of assistive technology. This grant enabled the development of an accessible Thunderbird user interface as well as its adoption through evangelism, mentoring, community-building, and technical leadership, with a particular focus on working with the jQuery community to implement ARIA support in this popular toolkit.

Grant Summary

  • 2006: $10,000
  • Focus: Accessibility
  • Recipient: Adaptive Technology Resource Centre
  • Location: Canada

More Information

This implementation constituted the first fully accessible free e-mail application. With the development of this technology, Thunderbird benefitted more completely from the accessibility of XUL and HTML. The coding effort attempted to address chrome and core bugs in the keyboard navigation and semantics.

This grant enabled resolution of some important accessibility issues in Thunderbird. Roughly 10 bugs have been fixed or had fixes nearing completion as part of this grant. The grant also enabled participation in the solving of accessibility issues as part of a greater team; including numerous patch reviews, triage discussions over IRC, as well as the reporting of new bugs.

Adaptive Technology Resource Centre

The ATRC is the University of Toronto's research and development effort to find innovative solutions to accessibility challenges. Providing training, consultation, and information, the ATRC helps both educators and users to effectively use adaptive technology.

Please consider making a contribution to the Mozilla Foundation so we can continue to make grants to other worthwhile organizations such as the ATRC.
