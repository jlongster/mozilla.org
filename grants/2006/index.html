2006 was the pilot year of the Mozilla Grants program. With a total of $282,500 in contributions for 2006, Mozilla funded the development of key accessibility features for Firefox and Thunderbird and enabled open source contributors to attend conferences such as FOSDEM and XTech. The majority of funding for this year went toward accessibility work, and our largest grant was a $50,000 contribution toward academic courses in open source technology at Seneca College in Toronto, Canada. Accessibility projects were the largest area of focus, comprising nearly half of the funds awarded to Mozilla grantees.

Summary of 2006 Grants

  • Community: $89,500
  • Education $50,000
  • Accessibility $125,000
  • Total: $282,500

Accessibility

  • $15,000 to WebAIM at Utah State University to develop XUL accessibility guidelines and an accompanying evaluation tool
  • $15,000 to Steve Lee for phase 1 of the Jambu project to optimize Firefox for pointer and switch access
  • $15,000 to Håkan Waara for phase 1 of a project to enhance accessibility of Firefox on OS X
  • $10,000 to Håkan Waara for phase 2 of a project to enhance accessibility of Firefox on OS X
  • $10,000 to Alexander Surkov to enhance accessibility of the optional XForms component of Firefox and other Mozilla-based products
  • $10,000 to the Adaptive Technology Resource Centre at the University of Toronto to implement accessibility features in the Thunderbird email client
  • $8,500 to Charles Chen to develop AJAX test cases for the WAI-ARIA standard for accessibility of rich Internet applications
  • $7,500 to Håkan Waara to produce an accessibility implementation plan for Firefox and other Mozilla products on OS X
  • $5,000 to Michael Gall to implement improvements to the accessibility of the DOM Inspector component used by used by developers of web-based applications for Firefox and other Mozilla-based products
  • $1,000 to Simon Harper for Mozilla sponsorship of the 2007 W4A conference.
  • $15,000 for travel expenses for participants in the 2006 Mozilla accessibility summit for developers of Mozilla-related accessibility software
  • $10,000 for travel expenses for participants, booth costs, and other expenses for Mozilla participation in the CSUN 2006 conference
  • $3,000 for travel expenses for participants in the W4A 2006 workshop.

Community Grants

  • $30,000 to the Mozdev Community Organization to support the mozdev.org site providing hosting of Mozilla-related open source projects
  • $30,000 to the Mitchell Kapor Foundation to sponsor a representative to the Internet Engineering Task Force to represent the interests of the open source community
  • $10,000 to Theo de Radt and the OpenBSD project in support of OpenSSH development and related activities
  • $10,000 to Henri Sivonen to develop an HTML5 conformance checker prototype
  • $7,500 to the Center for History and New Media at George Mason University to support phase I of the Mozilla Digital Memory Bank project
  • $15,000 for travel expenses for participants in the localizers meeting at FOSDEM 2006
  • $5,000 for travel expenses for participants in the XTech conference

Education Grants

  • $50,000 to Seneca College to support the work of David Humphrey as a liaison between the Mozilla project and educational institutions interested in teaching Mozilla-related courses
