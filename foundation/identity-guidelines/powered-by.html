Visual Identity Guidelines: Powered by Mozilla

Powered by Mozilla Logo Usage

The Powered by Mozilla logo is to be displayed in its entirety on either a solid light or dark background (avoid mid-tone or strongly colored backgrounds). The logo should not be displayed in parts, with color variations or with other elements superimposed on top.

Powered by Mozilla logo on white (52 KB) and dark (56 KB) background

The logo should not be displayed on screen at any smaller than 15px tall, and should follow the minimum clear space and color guidelines as shown below.

Minimum clear space and color guidelines

It's also important to note that, to avoid the appearance of unsanctioned partnerships or co-branding, the logo should not be paired with or placed near logos or other corporate entities without Mozilla's prior approval.

Linking

When displayed online, the Powered by Mozilla logo should link back to the Mozilla Foundation's "Powered by" overview page at http://www.mozilla.org/poweredby. This will give people a broader context of what the logo means and what Mozilla stands for.

Common Mistakes

The Powered by Mozilla logo should not be modified or displayed in ways that visually compromise it. Below are some common mistakes to avoid when using the logo:

Common Wordmark Mistakes (clockwise from top-left):
incorrect font color, incorrect font type, strongly colored or patterned background, introducing new elements or logos, non-standard orientation and sizing of text or incomplete or altered wordmark
